Good evening. This is **(your call sign)**, your net control station for this session of the Washington County Amateur Radio Emergency Service Net. This is a directed net. Those stations checking into the net are expected to monitor unless they request to be excused.

 

Regular sessions of this net meet Tuesdays at 7 p.m. local time except for meeting night, which is the third Tuesday of each month. This net is sanctioned to meet on the Western Oregon Radio Club, Inc. 145.450 MHz repeater with a 107.2 Hz tone which is our primary Net frequency. Our alternate frequency is the 440.350 MHz repeater with a 127.3 Hz tone.

 

Please refrain from using the word break unless you have a bona-fide emergency. Stations using the word break will be assumed to be indicating an emergency transmission.

 

These sessions are to provide ARES members training in the areas of net procedures and ARES drills. They also provide a brief time for announcements, comments, and questions by and for ARES members and interested visitors to the net. Emergency sessions of this net may be convened at the discretion of the Washington County Emergency Coordinator, the District Emergency Coordinator, or the ARRL Section Emergency Coordinator.
